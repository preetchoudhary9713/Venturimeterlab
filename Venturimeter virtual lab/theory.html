Aim

To determine the flow rate of a fluid through a pipe using the principle of conservation of energy, particularly Bernoulli's equation. To determine the coefficient of discharge for venturimeter.

Introduction

A Venturi meter is a device used to measure the flow rate of a fluid through a pipe. It works on the principle of Bernoulli's equation, which relates the pressure and velocity of a fluid in motion, and the principle of continuity, which states that the mass flow rate of a fluid is constant throughout a pipe.

Working Principle

The Venturi meter consists of a converging section, a throat (a section of reduced diameter), and a diverging section. As the fluid flows through the converging section, its velocity increases, and its pressure decreases. The pressure is at its minimum at the throat, where the velocity is at its maximum. As the fluid enters the diverging section, its velocity decreases, and its pressure increases, ideally recovering to the initial pressure.

By measuring the pressure difference between the wider section of the pipe and the throat, we can calculate the flow rate.

Formulae

Bernoulli's Equation (Simplified)

For an incompressible fluid flowing along a streamline:

P1 + (1/2)ρv12 = P2 + (1/2)ρv22

Where:

  • P1 and P2 are the pressures at points 1 and 2, respectively.
  • v1 and v2 are the velocities at points 1 and 2, respectively.
  • ρ is the density of the fluid.

Continuity Equation

For an incompressible fluid:

A1v1 = A2v2

Where:

  • A1 and A2 are the cross-sectional areas at points 1 and 2, respectively.

Flow Rate (Q)

Combining Bernoulli's and continuity equations, we can derive the formula for flow rate:

Q = A2 * √(2(P1 - P2) / ρ(1 - (A2/A1)2))

Where:

  • Q is the volumetric flow rate.

Coefficient of Discharge (Cd)

In real-world applications, the actual flow rate is often slightly lower than the theoretical flow rate due to energy losses. This is accounted for by the coefficient of discharge:

Qactual = Cd * Qtheoretical

Applications

Venturi meters are widely used in various applications, including:

  • Measuring flow rates in pipes and ducts.
  • Carburetors in internal combustion engines.
  • Medical applications, such as measuring blood flow.
